Commissioned in 1619 by Mughal Emperor Jahangir for his beloved wife, Nur Jahan, Shalimar Bagh ( meaning Abode of Love ) was more than a royal retreat. It was a poetic gift ‚Äî a physical embodiment of devotion, beauty, and peace.
Designed in the traditional Persian Charbagh style, the garden is split into three terraces, each with a purpose:

  • The outer garden (Diwan-i-Aam) was for public visitors.
  • The central terrace (Diwan-i-Khas) was for noblemen and private guests.
  • The uppermost terrace was reserved for the emperor and empress.
Even today, the gentle sound of fountains, the symmetry of flowing water channels (called chadar), and the shade of ancient chinar trees create an atmosphere that feels almost royal.
